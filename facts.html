Air-brushed out of history? An interesting insight from Elizabeth O’Farrell

#Irishwomen

In the image, from left to right, are Lieutenant John Lowe, Brigadier General Lowe and, facing him, Patrick Pearse. In the scene Pearse was standing in his greatcoat, with O’Farrell mostly obscured. O’Farrell’s feet and skirt can be seen behind Pearse, and the main part of her body was never invisible. This iconic photograph of surrender has been published widely. O’Farrell’s feet were deleted from some versions of the photograph, there has been a suggestion that women were literally ‘‘written out’’ of Irish history, but there was no underlying proof of gender-based or political agenda for this removal.
